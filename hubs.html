worlds(worlds)

3D, photogrammetry and playable worlds

In a time of global pandemic, climate change, and social unrest, San José State University’s Digital Media Art (DMA) students faced a new challenge during COVID-19: remote online learning. Beyond the gridded confines of Zoom, DMA students designed and built 3D immersive worlds in Mozilla Hubs, crafting experiences and interactions.

Teaching and learning converged in 1st person critiques inhabiting virtual worlds – bridging the digital divide through open source software Mozilla Hubs and Blender, increasing student engagement and access to participatory remote learning modalities.

In the space between the real and the virtual we found a range of emotions and outcomes, blurring the boundaries between work and play. The resulting Mozilla Hubs spaces fulfill the constructionist dream of learning-as-doing. Digital networks, artmaking, and mutualism converge in this genre-bending, Deleuze-meets-Freire dream of radical pedagogy, creativity, persistence and artmaking in a modern university.

The MozFest exhibition – experienced and created within Mozilla Hubs - features student works created in worlds (worlds) Spring 2020 – Spring 2023 by Digital Media Art (DMA) students in the CADRE Media Lab at San José State University.
